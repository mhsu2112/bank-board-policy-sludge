### Why these sources are relevant  

1. **AABD “Bank Director Regulatory Burden Report” (2014 & 2nd ed.)** – The only public compilation that enumerates **> 800** statutory, regulatory, and guidance provisions imposing duties on bank boards. It establishes the quantitative baseline for the “policy‑sludge” problem (see items 1, 3, 5, 10).  
2. **AABD/BuckleySandler Webinar (2016)** – Demonstrates how BSA/AML oversight alone forces boards to approve *more than 50* policies annually, illustrating specific overlapping duties.  
3. **AABD comment letters & “Additional Bank Board Regulatory Burdens Proposed”** – Provide concrete examples of *new* board‑certification requirements (FDIC § 370 attestation) that duplicate existing oversight functions.  
4. **OCC Guidelines & recent final rules (2026)** – Show agency‑level rulemaking that rescinds some duplicate data‑collection (Fair‑Housing Home‑Loan Data System) while still imposing numerous governance obligations (risk‑governance, independent‑director requirements).  
5. **Federal Reserve & FDIC statements (e.g., “Statement Concerning the Responsibilities of Bank Directors and Officers”)** – Outline the core fiduciary duties that underlie all other obligations, serving as the controlling authority for board responsibilities.  
6. **Basel III “end‑game” proposals & Bowman speech (2026)** – Highlight regulator‑driven attempts to *reduce* overlapping capital‑ratio calculations, directly addressing one facet of policy‑sludge.  
7. **GAO report (2024) & SIEPR policy brief (2025)** – Discuss the fragmented U.S. regulatory architecture that generates overlapping examinations and duplicative reporting requirements, reinforcing the systemic nature of the burden.  
8. **International guidance (Basel Committee, BIS) and IFC corporate‑governance guide** – Provide the *principle‑based* standards that U.S. agencies have incorporated, showing how multiple layers of guidance (international, federal, agency‑specific) converge on board duties.  

### Key take‑aways on “policy sludge”  

* **Volume:** > 800 distinct provisions (143 statutes + > 150 agency rules + hundreds of guidance items).  
* **Duplication:** Same duty (e.g., risk‑oversight, independent‑director composition) appears in OCC, FDIC, and FRB regulations, often with identical language but separate compliance check‑lists.  
* **Management‑in‑character duties:** Boards are required to *approve* granular policies that are more appropriately delegated to management (e.g., daily BSA/AML transaction monitoring procedures).  
* **Attestation creep:** Proposals to require board signatures on IT‑testing attestations or on each regulatory filing create personal‑liability exposure beyond the traditional fiduciary scope.  
* **Resource disparity:** Large institutions can staff compliance teams to meet the burdens; community banks face disproportionate cost‑to‑benefit ratios (CSBS surveys, AABD commentary).  

### Practical implication for banks  

* Conduct an **internal mapping** of each board duty to its statutory or regulatory source; flag duties that appear in multiple agencies’ rulebooks.  
* Where duplication exists, rely on the *most stringent* agency’s requirement but document the overlap to request **regulatory relief** (e.g., through EGRPRA decennial review).  
* Shift **management‑in‑character** items to senior‑management responsibility, documenting board oversight rather than direct approval (as recommended by AABD).  
* Monitor forthcoming **regulatory reform** (OCC 2026 community‑bank rules, Basel III end‑game proposals) for opportunities to reduce redundant obligations.  

This inventory provides a foundation for identifying and trimming the “policy sludge” that impairs board effectiveness while remaining anchored in the controlling U.S. federal authorities and the comprehensive AABD research that quantifies the burden.
