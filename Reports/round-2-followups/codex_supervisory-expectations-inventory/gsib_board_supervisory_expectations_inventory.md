# Current Supervisory Expectations for Boards of Category I U.S. GSIBs and IDI Subsidiaries

Citation-level working inventory focused on supervisory expectations, not binding regulatory obligations.

Current as of: June 5, 2026

Prepared for: bank directors, legal counsel, and policymakers evaluating duplicative, overlapping, or unduly burdensome board-level expectations.

## Scope and Method

This inventory captures current, in-force federal supervisory expectations directed to boards of directors of Category I U.S. global systemically important bank holding companies (GSIB BHCs) and their insured depository institution (IDI) subsidiaries. It draws from Federal Reserve, OCC, FDIC, CFPB, FFIEC, and interagency supervisory guidance, including SR letters, FILs, OCC bulletins and handbooks, FDIC manuals, CFPB examination procedures, and FFIEC manuals/handbooks.

This inventory intentionally excludes board-level legal obligations already addressed in the separate regulatory-obligations analysis, except where a supervisory source restates an obligation as part of examiner expectations or supervisory practice. In those cases, the row identifies the supervisory source and notes overlap where relevant.

## Current-Source Status Notes

| Status issue | Treatment in this inventory |
|---|---|
| Model risk management | The agencies issued revised interagency model risk guidance on April 17, 2026. Federal Reserve SR 26-2 supersedes SR 11-7 and SR 21-8; FDIC FIL-15-2026 rescinds FIL-22-2017 and FIL-27-2021; OCC Bulletin 2026-13 transmits the revised guidance. SR 11-7 is not treated as current. |
| OCC recovery planning | OCC rescinded Appendix E recovery-planning guidelines in 2026. OCC Appendix E is not inventoried as a current board expectation. Federal Reserve SR 14-8 recovery planning for LISCC firms remains inventoried. |
| FFIEC Cybersecurity Assessment Tool | FFIEC announced that the CAT would be sunset on August 31, 2025 and removed from the FFIEC website. CAT maturity-domain board language is not treated as current supervisory guidance. |
| Reputation risk | The Federal Reserve announced in 2025 that reputational risk would no longer be a component of examination programs. OCC and FDIC actions in 2025-2026, including OCC Bulletin 2026-23 and the OCC/FDIC final rule published April 10, 2026, remove reputation-risk references from supervisory materials. Older source language should be read with this limitation. |
| Climate risk principles | The 2023 interagency climate-related financial risk principles for large financial institutions are not included because the agencies withdrew them in 2025. |

| Non-executive directors | IC-2010 | Large banking organization compensation committee should be solely or predominantly non-executive directors, or non-executive directors should otherwise be actively involved in incentive-compensation oversight. |
| Parent/IDI overlapping directors | OCC-DIR affiliate/holding-company relationship section | Directors serving on both BHC and IDI boards must attend to subsidiary bank safety-and-soundness interests and document dissent where interests conflict. |

## High-Overlap Areas for Reform Review

1. Risk appetite is repeatedly assigned to the board across FRB SR21-3, FRB SR12-17, OCC Heightened Standards, OCC Corporate and Risk Governance, liquidity guidance, stress testing, capital planning, operational resilience, third-party risk, and compensation guidance.
2. Board approval of policies/programs appears across capital planning, stress testing, liquidity, BSA/AML, CMS, information security, third-party risk, incentive compensation, audit, and risk governance.
3. Board reporting expectations are layered across quarterly capital briefings, risk appetite monitoring, liquidity reports, third-party periodic reporting, compliance reports, BSA officer reports, audit reports, operational resilience surveillance, IT risk findings, and supervisory issue remediation.
4. Risk and audit committees are repeatedly used as the supervisory mechanism for independence and assurance: IRM stature, internal audit stature, CRE/CAE appointment and pay, third-party independent reviews, BSA independent testing, compliance audit, IT audit, and capital-planning deficiencies.
5. Compensation oversight is split across general board effectiveness, interagency incentive compensation, OCC compensation committee guidance, consumer compliance sales-practice procedures, and Heightened Standards performance-management requirements.
6. Director qualification and training expectations are distributed across OCC governance guidance, OCC Heightened Standards, FFIEC BSA/AML, CFPB CMS, and operational/IT/cyber guidance.
7. The most explicit inside/outside or independent-director requirements arise from OCC Heightened Standards, OCC audit-committee guidance, FFIEC BSA independent-testing procedures, Fed board-effectiveness guidance, and interagency incentive-compensation guidance.
