Board Duties at the Eight U.S. GSIBs:
Binding Obligations and Supervisory Expectations, 1987–2026

Three and a half decades of federal duties layered onto the boards of the eight U.S. globally systemically important banks, shown in two stacked layers. Binding obligations are statutes and rules with the force of law. Supervisory expectations are guidance, examination standards, and rating frameworks that shape examinations but do not, by themselves, carry the force of law. The two layers heavily overlap — and that duplication is itself part of the burden, so the chart stacks them in full rather than netting it out. The 2025–2026 inflection falls almost entirely on the expectations layer.
